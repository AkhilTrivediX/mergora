# Contributor Covenant 3.0 Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We pledge to make the Mergora community welcoming, safe, and equitable. Everyone who participates in good faith receives the same opportunity to contribute, regardless of race, ethnicity, caste, color, age, physical characteristics, neurodiversity, disability, sex or gender, gender identity or expression, sexual orientation, language, philosophy or religion, national or social origin, socioeconomic position, education, or other status.

## Encouraged behavior

Community members should:

1. respect the purpose of the project and its community spaces;
2. engage honestly and kindly;
3. respect different viewpoints and lived experiences;
4. accept responsibility for their actions and contributions;
5. give and receive constructive feedback with care;
6. work to repair harm when it occurs; and
7. act in ways that sustain the community's well-being.

## Restricted behavior

The following conduct, including threats or encouragement of it, violates this Code:

1. harassment or continued unwanted attention after a boundary is clearly stated;
2. insulting, demeaning, or pejorative attacks against a person or group;
3. stereotyping or discrimination based on identity or personal traits;
4. sexualized or inappropriately intimate conduct in a project context;
5. exposing or acting on another person's private information without permission;
6. causing, encouraging, or threatening violence or other harm; and
7. other behavior that threatens the safety or well-being of the community.

Impersonation, evading an enforcement action, failing to credit contributed sources, out-of-context promotion, and irresponsible presentation of harmful material are also prohibited.

Not every disagreement is a Code of Conduct violation. Good-faith technical disagreement, respectful criticism, and requests for evidence remain welcome.

## Reporting an issue

For a non-sensitive concern, use the [Mergora issue chooser](https://github.com/AkhilTrivediX/mergora/issues/new/choose). For a private report, use the repository's [private reporting form](https://github.com/AkhilTrivediX/mergora/security/advisories/new) and prefix the title with `Code of Conduct`. The repository owner monitors this confidential route.

Include only information relevant to the incident. Do not expose private information, credentials, or unrelated personal data. Reports will be acknowledged promptly, investigated with access limited to the people needed, and handled with safety and confidentiality as the priorities. Enforcement normally occurs privately; a broader notice may be made when it is safe, necessary, and consistent with the affected parties' privacy.

If a report concerns the current maintainer, submit it through the same private route. The maintainer will seek an independent reviewer before a final decision whenever practical and will document any unavoidable conflict of interest.

## Addressing and repairing harm

After reviewing impact, context, repetition, and safety, the maintainer may use this graduated response. Serious incidents may skip earlier steps.

1. **Warning** — A private written warning for an incident or related series of incidents. Repair may include acknowledging impact, apologizing, and clarifying future expectations.
2. **Temporarily limited activity** — A time-limited restriction on particular channels or interactions. Return should follow reflection, any agreed repair, and respect for the restriction.
3. **Temporary suspension** — Removal from project spaces for a defined period, with written conditions for return when a safe return is possible.
4. **Permanent ban** — Removal from all project spaces when repeated enforcement has failed or an incident is so severe that safe participation is no longer possible.

This ladder guides judgment; it does not prevent proportionate action needed to protect the community. Retaliation against a reporter, witness, affected person, or moderator is prohibited.

## Scope

This Code applies in Mergora's repository, documentation, discussions, issue tracker, releases, events, and other project spaces. It also applies when someone officially represents Mergora in public or private spaces.
